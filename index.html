About me

Currently, I am working as a postdoctoral research fellow in the Mittal group at Texas A&M University. Here I work on the role of protein-DNA interaction in chromatin compaction.

Previously, I worked as a postdoctoral fellow in the Thirumalai group at the University of Texas at Austin. I worked on optimizing parameters to generate force fields for intrinsically disordered proteins. I worked on FUS protein and the role of conformational fluctuation in fibril formation associated with neurodegenerative diseases.

I completed my Ph.D. under the guidance of Prof. Valeria Molinero in 2019 at the University of Utah. My graduate research focused on Statistical Mechanics, Self-Assembly, Porous Materials, and Nucleation.

During my undergraduate at Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, Pune, I did my research on the interaction of polymer with lipid bilayer membrane.

I did summer internships at University of Saarbruecken, Saarland where I worked on comparing the structure of nanoclusters and at the University of Surrey in UK, I worked on TD-DFT calculation to compute the CD spectra of neuropeptides.
